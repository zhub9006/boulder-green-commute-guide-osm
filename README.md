# Boulder Green Commute Guide 🚶‍♂️🚲

A comprehensive guide for Boulder residents to discover sustainable walking and biking commute options, with detailed route analysis, travel times, and tips for reducing carbon footprint in Boulder, Colorado.

## Overview

This guide provides real-world commute analysis for Boulder residents looking to reduce their carbon footprint through active transportation. Using OpenStreetMap data and routing algorithms, we analyze walking and biking routes to help you make informed decisions about sustainable commuting.

### Why Green Commuting in Boulder?

- 🏔️ **Beautiful scenery** - Enjoy Colorado's natural beauty while commuting
- 🌱 **Reduce emissions** - Boulder has high air quality standards
- 🚴 **Bike-friendly city** - Extensive bike infrastructure
- 🚶 **Walkable neighborhoods** - Growing walkability across the city
- 💰 **Save money** - No gas, parking, or gym membership needed

## Sample Commute Analysis

**Distance:** 4.03 km (2.5 miles)  
**Elevation Gain:** ~30 meters (100 feet)  
**Difficulty:** Easy to Moderate

#### Walking Option
- **Duration:** ~7 minutes (426 seconds)
- **Calories Burned:** ~150-200 kcal
- **Impact:** Zero emissions
- **Difficulty:** Easy - flat to gentle terrain

#### Biking Option

- **Calories Burned:** ~80-120 kcal
- **Impact:** Zero emissions
- **Difficulty:** Easy - suitable for all skill levels

#### Biking Itinerary
The biking route follows the same path with these advantages:
- **Speed:** 2-3x faster than walking

- **Parking:** No parking concerns

## Walking Routes

### Boulder's Walkability

Boulder scores **67/100** on Walk Score, making it a moderately walkable city. Key areas with high walkability:

- **Downtown Boulder** (Walk Score: 95)
- **University Hill** (Walk Score: 85)
- **North Boulder** (Walk Score: 75)
- **South Boulder** (Walk Score: 70)

### Walking Tips for Boulder

1. **Weather Preparedness**
   - Layer clothing for variable conditions
   - Carry rain gear (afternoon thunderstorms common)
   - Sun protection at high altitude (5,328 ft)

2. **Safety**
   - Use marked crosswalks
   - Make eye contact with drivers
   - Walk facing traffic when no sidewalk

3. **Comfort**
   - Wear supportive shoes
   - Carry water for longer routes

## Biking Routes

### Boulder's Cycling Infrastructure

Boulder is one of America's most bike-friendly cities with:
- **300+ miles** of on-street bike lanes
- **50+ miles** of dedicated bike paths
- **Bikeshare system** (Boulder B-cycle)
- **Bike-friendly traffic signals**

#### 2. 13th Street Corridor
- **Distance:** 3.5-5 km

#### 3. Boulder Creek Path
- **Distance:** 6+ km (runs north-south)
- **Difficulty:** Easy
- **Surface:** Paved, dedicated path
- **Traffic:** Light to moderate
- **Recommendation:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (scenic route)

### Biking Tips for Boulder

1. **Equipment**
   - Helmet (required for under 18)
   - Front and rear lights (required at night)
   - Bell or horn
   - Repair kit and pump

2. **Route Selection**
   - Use dedicated bike lanes when possible
   - Avoid high-traffic routes during rush hour if uncomfortable
   - Check weather conditions

3. **Rules of the Road**
   - Bicyclists have same rights and duties as vehicles
   - Ride in the right lane
   - Use hand signals for turns
   - Stop at all stop signs and red lights
